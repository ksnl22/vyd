Tirupati laddu

Tirupati Laddu is one of the most famous things in Andhra Pradesh and is also a recognizable prasadam. Although the Tirumala Venkateswara temple provides prasadam, or religious offerings, for its followers, you can buy it from the shops nearby. Moreover, this laddu includes gram flour, cashew nuts, cardamom, pure ghee, filtered sugar, and raisins as its primary ingredients.

Bobbatlu, also known as Puran Poli, is an all time favourite classic sweet dish prepared commonly in Andhra Pradesh and Maharashtra. It is considered to be very pious and is extensively prepared during important festivals and celebrations. The outer layer is prepared with wheat flour or refined flour and the inside stuffing is made with lentils and jaggery giving it a delicate and inviting taste.


pootharekulu

Pootharekulu is a traditional and delicate sweet from Andhra Pradesh, India, often referred to as the "paper sweet" due to its ultra-thin, translucent rice starch sheets that resemble delicate paper. The name comes from Telugu words Pootha meaning "coating" and Reku meaning "sheet." These thin layers are made by soaking and grinding rice into a fine batter, then spreading it over a hot surface to create paper-thin sheets. These sheets are then filled with a mixture of powdered sugar or jaggery, ghee (clarified butter), cardamom powder, and sometimes dry fruits like cashews, almonds, and pistachios, before being carefully folded or rolled into a crisp, melt-in-the-mouth sweet.


Kakinada Kaja

Kakinada Kaja is a renowned traditional sweet pastry originating from Kakinada in Andhra Pradesh, India. It is celebrated for its unique taste, texture, and cultural significance in the Telugu states.
